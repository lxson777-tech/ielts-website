Topic Vocabulary

Travel & Tourism

Word / PhraseMeaningExample in Context
overtourisma situation in which a destination receives more visitors than it can sustainably support"Overtourism has driven up living costs for residents of popular cities like Venice."
package holidaya pre-arranged trip that includes transport, accommodation, and other services for a single price"Package holidays remain popular among travellers seeking convenience."
eco-tourismtravel that aims to minimise environmental impact and support conservation"Eco-tourism has become an important source of income for developing countries."
cultural heritagethe traditions, monuments, and practices inherited from previous generations"Unregulated tourism can put fragile cultural heritage sites at risk."
infrastructurethe basic physical systems, such as transport and utilities, that support a country or region"Rapid growth in tourism has outpaced infrastructure development in many coastal towns."
visa restrictionslegal limitations on entry to a country imposed by its government"Loosened visa restrictions have led to a surge in international visitors."
off the beaten trackaway from popular tourist destinations, less commonly visited"Increasing numbers of travellers are seeking destinations off the beaten track."
public transporttransport services such as buses and trains available for use by the general public"Investment in public transport can significantly reduce a city's traffic congestion."
staycationa holiday spent in one's home country rather than travelling abroad"Rising airfares have made the staycation an increasingly popular alternative."
carbon-neutral traveltravel in which greenhouse gas emissions are offset or eliminated"Some airlines now offer carbon-neutral travel options for an additional fee."

Key Collocations

  • boost / drive the local economy
  • preserve cultural heritage
  • strain local infrastructure
  • venture off the beaten track
  • offset carbon emissions
  • a surge in visitor numbers

Useful Essay Phrases

  • "While tourism generates significant revenue, it can place an unsustainable strain on local resources."
  • "Overtourism has forced several cities to introduce visitor caps."
  • "Eco-tourism offers a model that balances economic benefit with environmental protection."
  • "Investment in infrastructure is essential to accommodate rising visitor numbers."
  • "Travellers are increasingly conscious of the environmental cost of long-haul flights."
Show Practice Exercise

Fill in the gaps using words from this section:

  1. Venice has become a well-known example of ________, with residents struggling to cope with visitor numbers. (overtourism)
  2. Many travellers now choose ________ over international trips to reduce their environmental impact. (staycations)
  3. ________ initiatives allow travellers to explore natural areas while minimising their ecological footprint. (Eco-tourism)
  4. Fragile ________ sites, such as ancient temples, are increasingly damaged by mass visitor numbers. (cultural heritage)
  5. Local governments often struggle to fund the ________ needed to support a growing tourism industry. (infrastructure)
