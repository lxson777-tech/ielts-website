Topic Vocabulary

Environment & Ecology

Word / PhraseMeaningExample in Context
carbon footprinttotal greenhouse gas emissions caused by an individual or activity"Governments are urging citizens to reduce their carbon footprint."
biodiversityvariety of plant and animal life in an ecosystem"Deforestation is one of the leading threats to biodiversity."
deforestationlarge-scale clearing of forests"Deforestation in the Amazon has accelerated at an alarming rate."
renewable energyenergy from sources that are naturally replenished (sun, wind, water)"Investment in renewable energy has surged over the past decade."
fossil fuelscoal, oil, and gas — non-renewable energy sources"Burning fossil fuels releases harmful greenhouse gases."
greenhouse gasesgases (CO2, methane) that trap heat in the atmosphere"Reducing greenhouse gas emissions is critical to limiting global warming."
sustainableable to be maintained without depleting natural resources"We need more sustainable approaches to agriculture and consumption."
ecosystema biological community of organisms and their environment"Human activity is disrupting fragile ecosystems worldwide."
pollutioncontamination of air, water, or soil by harmful substances"Air pollution in major cities poses a serious threat to public health."
conservationprotection and preservation of natural resources and wildlife"Conservation efforts have helped to stabilise tiger populations in India."

Key Collocations

  • tackle climate change / environmental problems
  • combat pollution / emissions
  • mitigate the effects of global warming
  • preserve natural habitats / wildlife
  • raise awareness about environmental issues
  • a pressing environmental concern

Useful Essay Phrases

  • "Environmental degradation poses a long-term threat to…"
  • "The most effective way to combat climate change would be to…"
  • "There is growing consensus that governments must…"
  • "Without urgent action, the consequences could be irreversible."
  • "Sustainable development must balance economic growth with…"
Show Practice Exercise

Fill in the gaps using words from this section:

  1. The dramatic loss of ________ in tropical rainforests is threatening thousands of species. (biodiversity)
  2. Scientists agree that ________ emissions must be reduced by at least 50% by 2050. (greenhouse gas)
  3. Many countries are now investing heavily in ________ sources such as solar and wind power. (renewable energy)
  4. Local authorities have introduced new initiatives to ________ the effects of air pollution in the city centre. (mitigate / combat)
  5. The ________ of ancient woodlands is essential for protecting endangered species. (conservation / preservation)

Topic Vocabulary

Technology & Society

Word / PhraseMeaningExample in Context
artificial intelligence (AI)computer systems that perform tasks requiring human-level intelligence"Artificial intelligence is transforming the healthcare and finance industries."
automationuse of machines/software to perform tasks previously done by humans"Automation has displaced many low-skilled manufacturing jobs."
digital divideinequality in access to digital technology and the internet"The digital divide remains a significant barrier to educational equality."
surveillanceclose monitoring of people, especially by governments or employers"Mass surveillance raises serious concerns about civil liberties."
data privacyprotection of personal information in digital systems"Tech companies must do more to protect users' data privacy."
innovationintroduction of new ideas, methods, or technologies"Technological innovation has driven unprecedented economic growth."
algorithma set of rules a computer follows to solve a problem"Social media algorithms can reinforce biased or extreme viewpoints."
misinformationfalse or inaccurate information spread unintentionally"The spread of misinformation online has become a major public concern."
cybersecurityprotection of computer systems from digital attacks"Investing in cybersecurity is now a national priority for most governments."
remote workingperforming job duties from home or a location outside the office"Remote working has fundamentally changed urban commuting patterns."

Key Collocations

  • rapid technological advancement / development
  • widespread adoption of technology
  • harness the potential of AI
  • pose a threat to privacy / employment
  • bridge the digital divide
  • regulate social media platforms

Useful Essay Phrases

  • "Technology has fundamentally transformed the way we…"
  • "While the benefits of automation are clear, the social costs cannot be ignored."
  • "Stricter regulation of social media is increasingly necessary because…"
  • "There is a real danger that AI will exacerbate existing inequalities."
  • "The challenge lies in harnessing the potential of technology while…"
Show Practice Exercise

Match each word to its definition:

  1. A system that monitors and gathers personal data from users' online activity → surveillance / algorithm
  2. The gap between those with internet access and those without → digital divide
  3. The replacement of human workers by machines → automation
  4. False information spread on social media → misinformation
  5. Protection of sensitive user information held by companies → data privacy

Topic Vocabulary

Health & Wellbeing

Word / PhraseMeaningExample in Context
obesitya medical condition involving excessive body fat that risks health"Childhood obesity rates have doubled in many countries over the past 20 years."
sedentary lifestylea way of life involving little physical activity"An increasingly sedentary lifestyle is contributing to a range of chronic illnesses."
mental healthpsychological and emotional wellbeing"Awareness of mental health issues has grown significantly in recent years."
preventable diseaseillness that could have been avoided through lifestyle or medical intervention"A large proportion of deaths in developed nations are caused by preventable diseases."
public healththe health of the population as a whole, managed by the government"Governments have a responsibility to invest in public health education."
healthcare systemthe organised system of hospitals, clinics, and professionals providing care"Underfunded healthcare systems struggle to cope with ageing populations."
nutritionthe process of obtaining food necessary for health and growth"Poor nutrition in childhood can have lasting consequences for development."
epidemic / pandemicwidespread occurrence of disease in a region / worldwide"The COVID-19 pandemic exposed deep inequalities in global healthcare."
wellbeingthe state of being comfortable, healthy, and happy"Employers are increasingly focusing on the wellbeing of their workforce."
life expectancythe average number of years a person is expected to live"Advances in medicine have dramatically increased life expectancy worldwide."

Key Collocations

  • tackle obesity / mental health issues
  • promote a healthy lifestyle / wellbeing
  • strain the healthcare system
  • chronic illness / disease / stress
  • deteriorating mental / physical health
  • allocate funding to healthcare / prevention

Useful Essay Phrases

  • "The rising incidence of obesity is placing enormous strain on…"
  • "Prevention is far more cost-effective than treatment."
  • "Mental health has historically been underfunded relative to physical health."
  • "Governments have a duty to create environments that promote healthy lifestyles."
  • "The long-term consequences of poor nutrition are well-documented."
Show Practice Exercise

Choose the correct word to complete each sentence:

  1. A increasingly ________ (sedentary / preventable) lifestyle means fewer people are doing regular exercise. (sedentary)
  2. Many experts argue that ________ (prevention / epidemic) campaigns are more effective than treating conditions after they develop. (prevention)
  3. Poor diet and lack of exercise are the leading causes of ________ (obesity / wellbeing) in children. (obesity)
  4. Workers who feel supported in their ________ (nutrition / wellbeing) are significantly more productive. (wellbeing)
  5. The ageing population will inevitably ________ (strain / promote) public healthcare systems in coming decades. (strain)

Topic Vocabulary

Education & Learning

Word / PhraseMeaningExample in Context
curriculumthe subjects and content taught at a school or university"Many argue the school curriculum should include more practical life skills."
academic achievementperformance measured by grades and qualifications"Academic achievement is only one measure of a student's potential."
critical thinkingthe ability to analyse information and form reasoned judgements"Critical thinking is increasingly valued by employers in all sectors."
tuition feesmoney charged for university education"Rising tuition fees are deterring students from lower-income families."
vocational trainingeducation focused on practical skills for a specific career"Vocational training programmes produce skilled workers who are immediately employable."
rote learningmemorisation of information through repetition without deep understanding"Critics argue that rote learning discourages creativity and problem-solving."
lifelong learningongoing, self-motivated learning throughout life"In a rapidly changing job market, lifelong learning has become essential."
equality of opportunitythe principle that all people should have the same access to education"Equality of opportunity remains an elusive goal in many education systems."
standardised testinguniform assessments used to measure student performance across schools"Standardised testing has been criticised for narrowing the curriculum."
literacy / numeracythe ability to read and write / work with numbers"Improving literacy and numeracy at primary level is the foundation of a strong education."

Key Collocations

  • broaden access to education
  • foster critical thinking / creativity
  • reform the education system
  • bridge the attainment gap
  • equip students with skills for the future
  • place excessive pressure on students

Useful Essay Phrases

  • "The purpose of education is not merely to transmit knowledge, but to…"
  • "There is growing evidence that standardised testing is counterproductive."
  • "Widening access to higher education is a matter of social justice."
  • "A well-rounded education should develop both academic and interpersonal skills."
  • "Investing in early childhood education yields the highest long-term returns."
Show Practice Exercise

Which word best completes each sentence? (curriculum / rote learning / vocational / tuition fees / critical thinking)

  1. Many countries are reconsidering their school ________ to include more digital skills. (curriculum)
  2. ________ training provides students with job-specific skills that are immediately applicable. (Vocational)
  3. Employers consistently rank ________ as one of the most valued graduate competencies. (critical thinking)
  4. The emphasis on ________ in some Asian education systems has been criticised for suppressing creativity. (rote learning)
  5. High ________ are creating a situation where a university degree is increasingly the preserve of the wealthy. (tuition fees)

Topic Vocabulary

Society, Culture & Globalisation

Word / PhraseMeaningExample in Context
globalisationthe process of increased interconnection between countries economically, culturally, and politically"Globalisation has brought both economic opportunity and cultural homogenisation."
inequalityan unequal distribution of resources, opportunities, or rights"Growing income inequality is destabilising societies around the world."
urbanisationthe process by which more people move from rural to urban areas"Rapid urbanisation in developing nations is creating severe infrastructure challenges."
cultural identitythe sense of belonging to a group based on shared culture, language, or tradition"Some fear that globalisation is eroding distinct cultural identities."
immigration / emigrationmovement of people into / out of a country"High levels of immigration have significantly contributed to economic growth."
social mobilitythe ability to move between social classes through effort or opportunity"Education is traditionally seen as the key driver of social mobility."
discriminationunfair treatment of a person based on race, gender, age, or other characteristics"Workplace discrimination remains a persistent problem in many industries."
consumerisma social and economic order based on the purchasing of goods and services"Critics argue that consumerism is driving unsustainable levels of resource use."
multiculturalismthe presence and positive promotion of multiple cultural communities in a society"Cities like London and Toronto are celebrated as examples of successful multiculturalism."
ageing populationa demographic shift where the proportion of elderly people increases"An ageing population creates long-term challenges for pension and healthcare systems."

Key Collocations

  • address inequality / discrimination
  • erode cultural identity / social cohesion
  • promote multiculturalism / integration
  • widen the wealth gap / social divide
  • cope with an ageing population
  • drive urbanisation / migration

Useful Essay Phrases

  • "Globalisation has had a profound impact on both economic development and cultural diversity."
  • "Social mobility has declined significantly in many Western nations."
  • "The tension between national identity and multiculturalism is a defining challenge of our time."
  • "Rapid urbanisation without adequate planning leads to poverty and social exclusion."
  • "Addressing inequality is not merely an ethical imperative — it is also economically rational."
Show Practice Exercise

Complete each sentence with the most appropriate word from this section:

  1. The ________ of manufacturing jobs to lower-wage countries is a direct consequence of globalisation. (migration / movement — accept "relocation")
  2. In cities experiencing rapid ________, housing shortages and inequality become major issues. (urbanisation)
  3. Despite laws against ________, women in many countries are still paid less than men for the same work. (discrimination)
  4. Many sociologists argue that ________ in modern societies is declining, meaning it is harder for people to improve their social standing. (social mobility)
  5. Some critics argue that ________ encourages wasteful behaviour and is environmentally damaging. (consumerism)
