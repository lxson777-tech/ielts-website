Topic Vocabulary

Government & Economy

Word / PhraseMeaningExample in Context
public expendituremoney spent by the government on services such as healthcare and education"Critics argue that public expenditure on defence should be redirected to education."
taxationthe system by which a government collects money from individuals and businesses"Progressive taxation is often proposed as a way to reduce inequality."
subsidyfinancial assistance given by the government to support an industry or activity"Government subsidies have accelerated the adoption of renewable energy."
inflationa general increase in prices and fall in the purchasing value of money"Persistent inflation has eroded household savings across the country."
cost of livingthe amount of money needed to cover basic expenses such as housing and food"The rising cost of living has pushed many families into financial hardship."
austerity measuresgovernment policies designed to reduce public spending, usually during economic difficulty"Austerity measures introduced after the recession led to significant cuts in public services."
welfare statea system in which the government provides for the economic and social wellbeing of citizens"Advocates of the welfare state argue it prevents extreme poverty."
privatisationthe transfer of a business, industry, or service from public to private ownership"Privatisation of the railway system sparked debate over service quality."
gross domestic product (GDP)the total value of goods and services produced by a country in a given period"GDP growth alone does not guarantee an improvement in citizens' quality of life."
public servicesservices provided by the government for the benefit of all citizens, such as healthcare and transport"Chronic underfunding of public services has led to longer waiting times nationwide."

Key Collocations

  • levy a tax
  • allocate public funds
  • curb / tackle inflation
  • cut public spending
  • widen the wealth gap
  • a strain on public finances

Useful Essay Phrases

  • "Governments must strike a balance between economic growth and social welfare."
  • "Higher taxation on the wealthy is often proposed as a solution to inequality."
  • "Austerity measures, while reducing debt, can disproportionately harm the most vulnerable."
  • "Investment in public services yields long-term economic benefits."
  • "Without government intervention, market forces alone rarely address inequality."
Show Practice Exercise

Fill in the gaps using words from this section:

  1. The government introduced ________ to reduce the national debt after the recession. (austerity measures)
  2. Rising ________ has made basic goods significantly more expensive for ordinary households. (inflation)
  3. Many voters are struggling with the increasing ________ in major cities. (cost of living)
  4. Critics of ________ argue that essential services should remain under public control. (privatisation)
  5. A rising ________ is generally seen as the clearest sign of economic growth. (gross domestic product / GDP)
