Diagram / Picture Labelling

What is it?

You are given a diagram (a drawing, map, or technical figure) with numbered blank labels. You must find the correct word(s) from the passage to fill each blank. The answer is always a word or words taken directly from the passage.

Key Rules

  • You must use words from the passage — do not paraphrase
  • Pay attention to the word limit: usually "no more than two words"
  • Spelling must be correct
  • The diagram shows a visual — your job is to connect parts of the image to terminology in the text

How to Approach It

  1. Study the diagram first. What is it showing? What parts are labelled and what are blank?
  2. Read the passage and identify the section that describes this diagram.
  3. Match each blank to the position on the diagram — think about location/function.
  4. Find the exact word(s) in the passage that name that part.
  5. Check the word limit — never exceed it.
⚠ Trap: writing paraphrases instead of exact passage words ⚠ Trap: exceeding the word limit ⚠ Trap: misspelling technical terms
Passage: About Ants

There are more than 10,000 known species of ants living throughout the world. Ants can be identified by the constriction that looks like a waist between the abdomen, at the end of the body, and the part of the body called the thorax, which is located before the head. Their bodies are covered with a hard armour called the exoskeleton. While the exoskeleton is their defensive weapon, their rear abdominal stinger is their offensive one. Like other insects, they have six legs; each with three joints. Ants have large heads with compound eyes, elbowed antennae, which act as sensors, and powerful pincers, known as mandibles. These pincers are an ant's most useful tool which are used for grabbing things, cutting and are a kind of trap-jaw.

There are three types of adult ants living in the colony: the queen, female ants, and male ants. Queen ants are the largest ants in the colony. They have wings and their purpose is to lay eggs. The queens can live up to 30 years. The ant life cycle has four stages of development: egg, larva, pupa and adult. The queen lays the eggs, which will hatch into larvae in a couple of days. The helpless larva are fed and groomed by the worker ants until they pass through the pupal stage. In a little more than a week, an adult ant will emerge and the metamorphosis is complete.
