Section 1 · Easiest

Everyday Conversation

What is it?

  • Conversation between two people in an everyday, transactional situation
  • Examples: booking a hotel, registering at a sports club, enquiring about a course
  • Typical question type: form / note completion
  • The answers are mostly names, numbers, addresses, dates and times

Why People Still Lose Marks Here

  • Spelling — names are spelled out letter by letter; write as you hear
  • -teen vs -ty: fourteen (14) sounds like "forTEEN"; forty (40) sounds like "FORty"
  • Corrections — "actually, make that Thursday" — the corrected version is the answer
  • Word limits — "no more than two words and/or a number" is enforced strictly

Strategy

  1. In the preview time, read the form and predict the type of each answer: a name? a number? a date?
  2. Follow the questions in order — answers come in sequence.
  3. Write as you listen; if you miss one, move on immediately.
  4. Dates can be written several ways: "the fifth of March" → 5th March or 5/3 — both acceptable.
Spelling counts Unlike Reading, a misspelled answer in Listening is marked wrong. Double-check proper nouns, numbers and addresses during the transfer time.
Simulation — Sports Centre Registration (read the transcript at a steady pace, as if listening; answer as you go)

Receptionist: Good morning, City Sports Centre. How can I help you?
Caller: Hi, I'd like to register for membership please.
Receptionist: Of course. Can I take your full name?
Caller: Yes, it's Sophie Hartmann. That's H-A-R-T-M-A-N-N.
Receptionist: Thank you. And your date of birth?
Caller: The fourteenth of March, 1992.
Receptionist: Postcode?
Caller: SW3 7BQ.
Receptionist: And which membership type would you like? We have Standard at £35 a month, or Premium at £55.
Caller: I'll take the Standard, please.
Receptionist: Great. Are there any specific facilities you're interested in?
Caller: Yes — mainly the swimming pool and yoga classes.
Receptionist: We run beginner yoga on Tuesdays at 6pm and intermediate on Thursdays at 7pm.
Caller: The Thursday class would suit me better.
Receptionist: Perfect. I'll send a confirmation to your email. Can you give me that?
Caller: It's s.hartmann at webmail dot co dot uk.
Receptionist: Wonderful. Your membership starts from Monday. You'll need to collect your card from the front desk between 9am and 5pm.
Caller: Thank you very much.
