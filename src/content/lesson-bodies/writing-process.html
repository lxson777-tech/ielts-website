Task 1 · Report

Process Diagrams

What is it?

You are shown a diagram of how something is made or how something works — a natural cycle (the water cycle, a butterfly's life) or a man-made process (recycling glass, producing electricity). You describe every stage in order, in at least 150 words.

How It Differs from Charts

  • There are usually no numbers — the challenge is grammar, not data selection
  • Use the present simple, mostly in the passive voice for man-made processes
  • The overview states how many stages there are, where the process begins and ends, and whether it is cyclical or linear
  • You must mention every stage — skipping one hurts Task Achievement

Reading the Diagram — What to Look For

Before writing, spend two minutes answering these questions — they give you the whole report (the four-paragraph method itself is in How to Answer Task 1):

  1. How many stages are there? Count them — this number goes straight into your overview.
  2. Where does it start and end? First input, final output — the other half of the overview.
  3. Linear or cyclical? Does it finish, or loop back to the beginning?
  4. Natural or man-made? Natural processes stay active (the water evaporates); man-made ones take the passive (the glass is crushed).
  5. Where will you split the stages? Find the natural halfway point — that's the break between your two detail paragraphs.
  6. Any branches or simultaneous steps? Flag them with meanwhile / at the same time.

Key Language

JobLanguage
SequencingFirst / To begin with / At the initial stage / Next / Following this / Subsequently / After that / Finally
Passive voicethe bottles are collected / the mixture is heated / the products are then delivered
Purposein order to remove impurities / so that it can be reused
Simultaneitymeanwhile / at the same time / during this stage
Cyclesthe cycle then repeats / returns to the first stage / an ongoing, natural cycle

Natural processes usually stay in the active voice (the water evaporates); man-made processes prefer the passive (the glass is crushed) because the doer is unimportant.

Example question

The diagram below shows how glass bottles are recycled into new products. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features.
Show model report (opening paragraphs)

The diagram illustrates the process by which used glass bottles are converted into new glass items.

Overall, recycling involves seven stages, beginning with the collection of discarded bottles and ending with the sale of products made from reclaimed glass. It is a continuous cycle rather than a one-off procedure.

At the first stage, empty bottles are deposited at collection points, from where they are transported to a recycling plant. There, they are washed in high-pressure water in order to remove any impurities, before being sorted by colour into clear, green and brown glass…


  • Overview counts the stages and names the start and end ✓
  • Passive voice throughout (are deposited, are transported, are washed) ✓
  • Sequencers link every stage (at the first stage, from where, before) ✓

Common Mistakes

⚠ Forgetting the overview because "there are no trends" — count the stages instead ⚠ Active voice everywhere ("someone collects the bottles") ⚠ Skipping stages or inventing extra ones ⚠ Only using "then… then… then" to sequence
