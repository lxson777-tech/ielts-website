Topic Vocabulary

Crime & Law

Word / PhraseMeaningExample in Context
juvenile delinquencycriminal behaviour committed by young people"Rising juvenile delinquency has prompted calls for earlier intervention programmes."
deterrentsomething that discourages a person from committing an act, especially through fear of consequences"Harsher sentencing is often proposed as a deterrent to violent crime."
rehabilitationthe process of helping an offender return to a law-abiding life"Prisons that prioritise rehabilitation tend to have lower reoffending rates."
recidivismthe tendency of a convicted criminal to reoffend"High recidivism rates suggest that short prison sentences do little to change behaviour."
white-collar crimenon-violent crime committed for financial gain, typically by professionals"White-collar crime such as fraud often goes undetected for years."
law enforcementthe government agencies and officials responsible for enforcing the law"Increased funding for law enforcement has not always led to lower crime rates."
capital punishmentthe legal execution of a person as punishment for a crime"Public opinion on capital punishment remains sharply divided."
custodial sentencea punishment involving imprisonment"Judges are increasingly favouring community service over short custodial sentences."
socioeconomic factorssocial and economic conditions, such as poverty, that influence behaviour"Many criminologists argue that socioeconomic factors are the root cause of crime."
petty crimeminor offences such as shoplifting or vandalism"Petty crime tends to rise in areas with high youth unemployment."

Key Collocations

  • commit a crime / an offence
  • tackle / curb rising crime rates
  • serve a sentence
  • address the root causes of crime
  • reoffend after release
  • a zero-tolerance approach

Useful Essay Phrases

  • "Proponents of harsher punishment argue that it acts as an effective deterrent."
  • "Others maintain that rehabilitation is more effective than punishment alone."
  • "Without addressing the underlying socioeconomic causes, crime rates are unlikely to fall."
  • "There is little empirical evidence that capital punishment reduces violent crime."
  • "A combination of prevention, punishment, and rehabilitation offers the most balanced approach."
Show Practice Exercise

Fill in the gaps using words from this section:

  1. Many criminologists believe that ________ such as poverty and unemployment are the root cause of crime. (socioeconomic factors)
  2. Prisons that focus on ________ rather than pure punishment tend to see lower reoffending rates. (rehabilitation)
  3. Community programmes have been shown to reduce ________ among first-time young offenders. (juvenile delinquency)
  4. ________ such as fraud and embezzlement can cause enormous financial harm despite being non-violent. (White-collar crime)
  5. High rates of ________ suggest that many prisons fail to prepare inmates for life after release. (recidivism)
