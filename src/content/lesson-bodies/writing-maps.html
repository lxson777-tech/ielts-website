Task 1 · Report

Maps & Plans

What is it?

You are shown two maps of the same place at different times (e.g. a town in 1980 and today), or occasionally a plan of a building before and after redevelopment. Your job is to describe the changes: what was added, removed, replaced or expanded.

What the Examiner Wants

  • An overview of the overall transformation (e.g. "the town became far more built-up and tourist-oriented")
  • Changes described with the right tenses: past → present = past simple + present perfect
  • Location language so the reader can follow the map without seeing it
  • Logical organisation. By area of the map, or by type of change

Key Language

JobLanguage
Locationin the north-east of / on the southern edge / adjacent to / opposite / alongside the river / in the centre
Additionsa marina was constructed / a shopping centre has been built / new housing appeared
Removalsthe forest was cut down / the factory was demolished / the fields disappeared
Replacementthe farmland was converted into a golf course / gave way to apartments / was replaced by offices
Expansionthe harbour was extended / the road was widened / the village expanded considerably

Reading the Maps. What to Look For

Two minutes of systematic comparison gives you everything (the four-paragraph method itself is in How to Answer Task 1):

  1. Check the dates. Past → past, past → present decides your tenses (past simple + present perfect).
  2. Find north and the main fixed reference points. The river, the main road, the coast.
  3. Scan for the four kinds of change: what disappeared, what appeared, what changed use (farmland → housing), what grew or shrank.
  4. Name the headline transformation for the overview: more built-up? more tourist-oriented? countryside lost to development?
  5. Note what stayed the same: unchanged features are worth a sentence and examiners reward spotting them.
  6. Split the map for your two detail paragraphs: by area (north vs south, coast vs inland) or by change type (new construction vs demolition).
Example question

The maps below show the town of Riverside in 1980 and the same town today. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.
Show model report (opening paragraphs)

The two maps compare the layout of Riverside in 1980 with how the town looks today.

Overall, the town has been dramatically modernised and enlarged over the period. Almost all of its agricultural land has been developed, while the riverfront, once industrial, is now devoted to leisure.

The most striking changes are along the river. The warehouses that lined the northern bank in 1980 were demolished and replaced by a park and a marina, and the old stone bridge was widened to carry the new ring road. Directly opposite, on the southern bank, a row of restaurants has been built where the fish market once stood…


  • Overview gives the headline transformation, no map detail ✓
  • Past simple + present perfect handled correctly ✓
  • Location phrases guide the reader (along the river, northern bank, directly opposite) ✓

Common Mistakes

⚠ Describing each map separately instead of the changes between them ⚠ Compass confusion. Check north before you write ⚠ Present tense for things that happened between the two dates ⚠ Ignoring features that did not change
