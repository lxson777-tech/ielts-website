Discussion Essays

What is it?

The prompt presents two opposing views and asks you to "Discuss both views and give your own opinion." Three jobs, all compulsory: explain view A fairly, explain view B fairly, and make your own position clear. (The four-paragraph method itself is covered in How to Answer Task 2 — this lesson is about adapting it to discussion questions.)

How It Differs from Opinion Essays

  • You must cover both views — one-sided essays lose Task Response marks
  • Discuss the view you disagree with respectfully: explain why people hold it, then show its limits
  • Your own opinion can match one view, or sit between them — but it must be explicit, not implied
  • Signal whose voice each sentence is: supporters argue… / critics point out… / in my view…

Structure (4–5 paragraphs)

  1. Introduction: paraphrase both views + state your opinion: "While some see university as job preparation, others value knowledge for its own sake. I side largely with the latter."
  2. Body 1: the first view — why people hold it, with an example.
  3. Body 2: the second view, same depth. Don't let your preferred view get all the space.
  4. Body 3 (optional): your own position developed, especially if it blends the two.
  5. Conclusion: summarise both sides in a phrase and restate where you stand.

Voice-Signalling Language

JobLanguage
View AProponents argue that / Those in favour claim / It is often said that
View BOpponents counter that / Critics point out / Others take the view that
WeighingThere is merit in both positions / The stronger argument, however,
Your voiceIn my view / I am inclined to agree with / My own position is that

Without these signals the examiner cannot tell whether a sentence is your opinion or a view you are reporting — and an unclear position caps Task Response at Band 6.

Example question

Some people think the main purpose of university education is to prepare students for employment, while others believe it should develop knowledge for its own sake. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.
Show model introduction

Opinion is divided on what universities are for: one camp regards them primarily as a pipeline into the workforce, while the other believes their true role is the pursuit of understanding regardless of its market value. This essay will examine both positions, and argue that while employability matters, it should remain a by-product of education rather than its purpose.


  • Both views paraphrased in one sentence ✓
  • Opinion explicit, and nuanced — a blend, clearly owned ✓
  • Roadmap tells the examiner exactly what is coming ✓

Common Mistakes

⚠ Discussing only the view you support ⚠ Forgetting to give your own opinion at all ⚠ Straw-manning the view you dislike in one dismissive sentence ⚠ Unsignalled voices — whose opinion is this sentence?
