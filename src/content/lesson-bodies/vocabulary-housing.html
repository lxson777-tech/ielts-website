Topic Vocabulary

Housing & Urban Life

Word / PhraseMeaningExample in Context
affordable housinghousing priced within the means of low- to middle-income households"The shortage of affordable housing has become a defining issue in major cities."
urban sprawlthe uncontrolled expansion of a city into surrounding undeveloped areas"Urban sprawl often leads to greater reliance on cars and longer commutes."
gentrificationthe process by which a poorer area is transformed by an influx of wealthier residents"Gentrification has pushed many long-term residents out of the neighbourhood."
property marketthe buying, selling, and renting of land and buildings"The property market has cooled significantly following interest rate rises."
high-rise developmentthe construction of tall multi-storey buildings, typically to increase housing density"High-rise development is often proposed as a solution to urban land shortages."
commutethe regular journey between home and workplace"Long commutes are frequently cited as a major source of daily stress."
green spaceparks, gardens, or other natural areas within an urban environment"Access to green space has been linked to improved mental health in city dwellers."
homelessnessthe state of having no home or permanent place to live"Rising rents have contributed to a sharp increase in homelessness."
mixed-use developmenturban planning that combines residential, commercial, and recreational spaces in one area"Mixed-use development is designed to reduce the need for long commutes."
zoning lawsgovernment regulations that control how land in a specific area may be used"Strict zoning laws can slow down the construction of much-needed housing."

Key Collocations

  • tackle the housing crisis
  • drive up property prices
  • relax / tighten zoning laws
  • revitalise a neighbourhood
  • ease traffic congestion
  • a densely populated area

Useful Essay Phrases

  • "The chronic shortage of affordable housing has pushed many young people out of major cities."
  • "Gentrification often improves an area economically while displacing its original residents."
  • "High-rise development can ease land shortages, but risks eroding a city's character."
  • "Mixed-use development reduces the need for long, stressful commutes."
  • "Without access to green space, urban residents' wellbeing can suffer significantly."
Show Practice Exercise

Fill in the gaps using words from this section:

  1. The lack of ________ has forced many young families to move further from city centres. (affordable housing)
  2. ________ has transformed the once run-down district into one of the city's most expensive areas. (Gentrification)
  3. Uncontrolled ________ has swallowed up farmland on the outskirts of the city. (urban sprawl)
  4. Access to ________ such as parks is essential for residents' physical and mental health. (green space)
  5. Rising rents and stagnant wages have contributed to a sharp rise in ________. (homelessness)
