Topic Vocabulary

Family & Relationships

Word / PhraseMeaningExample in Context
nuclear familya family unit consisting of parents and their children living together"The traditional nuclear family is becoming less common in many societies."
extended familya family that includes relatives beyond parents and children, such as grandparents and cousins"In many cultures, the extended family plays a central role in child-rearing."
childcarethe care and supervision of children, especially while parents are working"The high cost of childcare discourages many parents from returning to work."
single-parent householda family in which one parent raises children without a partner"Single-parent households often face greater financial pressure than two-parent families."
generation gapthe difference in attitudes and values between older and younger generations"The generation gap has widened as younger people adopt new technologies faster than their parents."
household choresdomestic tasks such as cleaning and cooking shared among family members"Studies show that household chores are still unevenly divided between men and women."
parental leavetime off work granted to parents following the birth or adoption of a child"Extending paid parental leave has been shown to benefit both parents and children."
quality timetime spent giving full attention to a relationship or activity, especially with family"Busy work schedules leave many parents with little quality time for their children."
filial responsibilitythe duty of children to care for their ageing parents"In many Asian cultures, filial responsibility remains a deeply held value."
peer pressuresocial pressure from members of one's own age group to behave in a certain way"Peer pressure plays a significant role in shaping teenagers' decisions."

Key Collocations

  • raise / bring up a child
  • juggle work and family life
  • instil values in children
  • succumb to peer pressure
  • share household chores
  • a close-knit family

Useful Essay Phrases

  • "The structure of the family has changed considerably over the past few decades."
  • "Working parents often struggle to find sufficient quality time for their children."
  • "In many Western societies, filial responsibility has increasingly shifted to the state."
  • "Generous parental leave policies can ease the burden on new parents."
  • "Peer influence often rivals parental influence during adolescence."
Show Practice Exercise

Fill in the gaps using words from this section:

  1. The rising cost of ________ has made it difficult for many parents to return to full-time work. (childcare)
  2. ________ households now make up a significant proportion of families in many countries. (Single-parent)
  3. Many teenagers give in to ________ when deciding how to dress or behave. (peer pressure)
  4. The ________ between grandparents and grandchildren can make communication about technology difficult. (generation gap)
  5. In several Asian societies, ________ means adult children are expected to support their ageing parents. (filial responsibility)
