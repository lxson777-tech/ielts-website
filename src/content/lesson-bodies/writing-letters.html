Task 1 · General Training

Letters

What is it?

General Training candidates write a letter of at least 150 words responding to a everyday situation — complaining, requesting, apologising, inviting, or giving information. The prompt always contains three bullet points, and your letter must cover all three.

The Three Tones

  • Formal — someone you don't know, in authority (a manager, a company). Dear Sir or Madam … Yours faithfully
  • Semi-formal — someone you know in a professional or neighbourly role. Dear Mr Chen … Yours sincerely / Kind regards
  • Informal — a friend or family member. Dear Sam … Best wishes / Take care

Structure

  1. Greeting matched to the tone.
  2. Opening paragraph: why you are writing — "I am writing to…" (formal) or "Just a quick note to…" (informal).
  3. One short paragraph per bullet point — three bullets, three paragraphs. Develop each with a detail or example; don't just restate the bullet.
  4. Closing line: the action or feeling you want to leave — "I look forward to your reply." / "Can't wait to see you!"
  5. Sign-off matched to the greeting.

Tone Phrasebook

FunctionFormalInformal
RequestingI would be grateful if you could…Could you do me a favour and…?
ComplainingI wish to express my dissatisfaction with…I'm really not happy about…
ApologisingPlease accept my sincere apologies for…I'm so sorry about…
SuggestingMay I suggest that…How about…?
ThankingI greatly appreciate your assistance with…Thanks a million for…

Consistency matters more than fanciness: one "Hey!" in a formal complaint, or one "I would be most obliged" to your best friend, and your Task Achievement band drops.

Example question

You are planning to hold a party at your home, and you are worried the noise may disturb your neighbour. Write a letter to your neighbour. In your letter: explain the reason for the party · describe what you will do to limit the noise · invite them to attend.
Show model letter (semi-formal, opening)

Dear Mrs Patel,

I hope this note finds you well. I'm writing to let you know that we will be hosting a small gathering at our house next Saturday evening, and I wanted to tell you about it in advance.

The occasion is my daughter's graduation — after four long years, she has finally finished her degree, and the family are coming together to celebrate her achievement…


  • Tone fits a neighbour you know politely: friendly but not casual ✓
  • Purpose stated in the first paragraph ✓
  • First bullet (the reason) developed with specifics, not restated ✓

Common Mistakes

⚠ Missing or rushing one of the three bullet points ⚠ Mixed tone — formal opening, informal body ⚠ "Yours faithfully" after "Dear Mr Chen" (it pairs with "Dear Sir or Madam") ⚠ No sign-off, or signing a full real name on an informal letter
