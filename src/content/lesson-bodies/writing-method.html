Task 1 · The Universal Method

One Approach for Every Task 1

What Task 1 Really Asks

  • It is a report, not an essay: you describe what the visual shows, factually
  • 20 minutes, at least 150 words (aim for 170-190)
  • Worth a third of your Writing marks: Task 2 counts double
  • You select the key features; you never list every number
  • No opinion, no explanation of causes, no conclusion: only what the data shows

The Same Method Every Time

Whether you get a bar chart, line graph, pie chart, table, process diagram or map, the report has the same four paragraphs. Learn the method once, then each type lesson tells you what to look for in that particular visual.

(General Training letters follow their own format. See the Letters lesson.)

The Four Paragraphs

ParagraphJobHow
1 · Introduction
(1 sentence)		 Say what the visual shows, in your own words Paraphrase the question: shows → compares / illustrates; the percentage of → the proportion of. Keep the place, the units and the time period. Never copy the question wording.
2 · Overview
(2-3 sentences)		 The most important paragraph: the big picture Start with "Overall,". Give the 2-3 key features: the main trend, the biggest contrast, the end state. No numbers. Without a clear overview your Task Achievement is capped around Band 5.
3 · Detail 1 First logical group of information Group the data (e.g. the categories that rose / the first half of the process / one area of the map) and support with selected figures.
4 · Detail 2 The remaining group Cover everything the task requires. Every line, stage or area must be mentioned, even if briefly. Compare across groups where you can.

The 20-Minute Plan

  1. Minutes 1-3. Analyse and plan. What type is it? What period (which tense)? What are the 2-3 key features? How will you split the details into two groups? Do this before writing a word.
  2. Minutes 4-17. Write the four paragraphs. Around 13 sentences total is plenty.
  3. Minutes 18-20. Check. Word count over 150? Overview present and number-free? Tense consistent with the time period? Figures copied accurately?

How the Four Criteria Apply to Task 1

Criterion (25% each)What it means here
Task AchievementA clear overview, all key features covered, data reported accurately, 150+ words, right format (report, not letter or essay)
Coherence & CohesionThe four-paragraph shape itself, logical grouping, and natural linking (while, whereas, in contrast, respectively)
Lexical ResourcePrecise data language (trend verbs, graded adverbs, proportion phrases) used accurately, not decoratively
Grammatical Range & AccuracyCorrect tense for the period, comparatives, passives (for processes), complex sentences without errors
Golden rules Never copy the question sentence. Never skip the overview. Never invent reasons for the data. Never write a conclusion. Never spend more than 20 minutes. Task 2 is worth twice as much.
✓ Past period → past simple; "today" or no dates → present simple ✓ Two visuals? Compare them. Don't describe them separately ⚠ Under 150 words = automatic Task Achievement penalty
