Monologue & Maps

What is it?

  • One speaker in a non-academic, social context
  • Examples: a guide describing a facility, a radio presenter announcing an event, an induction talk
  • Typical question types: map / plan labelling, multiple choice, matching
  • No conversation to follow — but also no second voice to give you a pause

The Map Task

  • Study the map before the recording starts: find the entrance, labelled landmarks, and the blanks
  • The speaker almost always moves through the map in one direction — follow with your finger
  • Answers are locations relative to landmarks, so the location language below is the whole game

Location Language

opposite / across from next to / beside / adjacent to between X and Y at the end of on the corner of to the north/south/east/west of turn left/right at in the centre near / close to / not far from

Strategy

  1. Use the preview time on the map first, options second.
  2. Listen for signpost language: "Now, as you come in…", "Moving along the corridor…", "Just past the café…"
  3. For multiple choice, read the options and note how they differ — the speaker will mention several; only one fits the question.
  4. Don't lose your place: if you miss a label, jump to the next one immediately.
Simulation — Community Centre Tour (read at a steady pace, as if listening)

Guide: Welcome to the Riverside Community Centre. Let me quickly point out where everything is before we start. We're standing at the main entrance now. Directly opposite the entrance, across the hall, you'll see the reception desk — any questions during your visit, that's the place to go. To the left of reception is the café, which serves lunch between midday and two. If you walk down the corridor to the right of reception, the first room you come to is the library — that used to be the games room, but it was converted last spring. Just past the library, at the end of the corridor, is the fitness studio. Classes run every evening, and you can book at reception. Finally, if you go through the double doors between the café and reception, you'll find the main hall, where this afternoon's event takes place.

Where is each place? Choose from the descriptions (one is not used)

  1. Reception desk: _______________
  2. Café: _______________
  3. Library: _______________
  4. Fitness studio: _______________
  5. Main hall: _______________

A) at the end of the right-hand corridor   B) opposite the main entrance   C) through the doors between the café and reception   D) first room along the right-hand corridor   E) to the left of reception   F) next to the main entrance

Show Answers
  1. B — "directly opposite the entrance"
  2. E — "to the left of reception"
  3. D — "the first room you come to" down the right corridor
  4. A — "just past the library, at the end of the corridor"
  5. C — "through the double doors between the café and reception" (F is the unused distractor)
