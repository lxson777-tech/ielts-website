Test Format

What is Writing Task 1?

The Basics

  • Part of a 1-hour writing test (Task 1 + Task 2)
  • Recommended time: no more than 20 minutes
  • Minimum word count: 150 words (aim for 170–190)
  • Worth 33% of your total writing marks
  • It is a report, not an essay
  • You describe data, a process, a map, or a diagram

Important Rules

  • You can do Task 2 first if you prefer — you manage your own time
  • Never write a conclusion — write an overview instead
  • Do not give your opinion — only describe what the data shows
  • Do not copy the question wording — paraphrase it in your introduction
  • Always include an overview — it is the most important paragraph
Report vs. Essay — the key difference In Task 2 you write an essay with an introduction, body paragraphs, and a conclusion. In Task 1 you write a report with an introduction, an overview, and body paragraphs. There is NO conclusion in Task 1. The examiner is explicitly looking for an overview, not a conclusion.
2
Task Types

Six Types of Task 1

You can receive any of the six task types below — or occasionally a combination of two (e.g. a bar chart alongside a pie chart). Each type requires slightly different language, but the report structure is always the same.

📊

Bar Chart

Compares categories. May show change over time or compare groups (e.g. men vs. women).

📈

Line Graph

Shows trends over a period of time. Requires increase/decrease vocabulary.

🥧

Pie Chart

Shows proportions of a whole. Often compare/contrast or change over time.

📋

Table

Data in rows and columns. Select key data — don't list everything.

⚙️

Diagram / Process

Shows how something works or is made. Use passive voice for stages.

🗺️

Map

Describes the layout of a town or area. Often compares two time periods.

Which Tense to Use

  • Past data (e.g. 1990–2010) → use past simple: "Sales rose sharply in 2005."
  • Present data / no dates → use simple present: "The chart shows that…"
  • Future predictions (projected data) → use future forms: "Numbers are expected to rise."
  • Diagrams / processes → use present simple passive: "The material is heated and then filtered."
  • Maps → past simple for old map, present for current: "The factory was replaced by a park."
⚠ Don't mix past and present for the same data set ⚠ Check the dates on the chart before you start writing

Marking Criteria

How You Are Scored

There are four criteria, each worth 25% of your Task 1 score.

25%
Task Achievement

Did you cover all key features? Is there a clear overview? Did you write 150+ words?

25%
Coherence & Cohesion

Is the report well-structured with clear paragraphs? Are linking words used well?

25%
Lexical Resource

Do you use a range of vocabulary accurately? Are words spelled correctly?

25%
Grammatical Range & Accuracy

Do you use a mix of sentence structures with few grammatical errors?

Band Score Benchmarks for Task Achievement (Overview)

  • Band 5 — key features are very limited or there is no overview
  • Band 6 — a relevant overview is attempted but not done well
  • Band 7 — a clear overview is presented
  • Band 8 — key features are skilfully selected in a strong overview
⚠ No overview = Band 5 maximum for Task Achievement ✓ A clear overview = immediate Band 7 for Task Achievement

Report Structure

How to Structure Your Report

Every Task 1 report — regardless of task type — follows the same 4-paragraph structure.

Para 1
Introduction

Paraphrase the chart description given in the question. Say what the chart/graph/diagram shows. Do NOT copy the question — rewrite it in your own words. Usually 1–2 sentences.

Para 2
Overview — Most Important Paragraph

Identify and summarise the 2–3 most significant overall trends or features. Do NOT include specific figures here. This is the big picture. Start with "Overall," or "In general,".

Para 3
Body Paragraph 1

Describe the first group of detailed data with specific figures, dates, and comparisons. Support the key features mentioned in your overview with numbers and facts.

Para 4
Body Paragraph 2

Describe the second group of data. Continue supporting the overview with more specific details. A third body paragraph is possible but not usually necessary.

Where does the Overview go? The overview is always the second paragraph — immediately after the introduction. Some students put it at the end, thinking it's like a conclusion. This is wrong. An overview is not a conclusion. It summarises what the data shows overall, placed early so the reader knows what to expect.

Introduction: Paraphrasing the Question

The question will give you a description like: "The graph below shows the number of tourists visiting three countries between 2000 and 2020."

You must paraphrase — change words and structure, not just one word.

✗ Wrong — Copied
"The graph below shows the number of tourists visiting three countries between 2000 and 2020."

Copying the question word-for-word is penalised. Those words are not counted toward your word total either.

✓ Correct — Paraphrased
"The line graph illustrates how tourist numbers to three different countries changed over a twenty-year period from 2000 to 2020."

Key changes: "shows" → "illustrates", "visiting" → "tourist numbers to", "between" → "over a period from".

Overview: What to Include

  1. Look at the chart and ask: What are the most obvious trends?
  2. Pick the 2–3 most significant features — not all of them
  3. Write them in general terms — no specific numbers in the overview
  4. Start with: "Overall," / "In general," / "It is clear that…"
✓ "Overall, Country A consistently attracted the most visitors throughout the period."
⚠ "Overall, Country A had 5 million visitors in 2000." — this has a number, don't do this

Vocabulary

Essential Task 1 Vocabulary

Upward Trends

  • to rise / a rise
  • to increase / an increase
  • to climb / a climb
  • to grow / a growth
  • to go up
  • to rocket / to soar (dramatic increases only)

Downward Trends

  • to decrease / a decrease
  • to drop / a drop
  • to fall / a fall
  • to decline / a decline
  • to go down
  • to dip / a dip (brief drop before recovering)
SituationVocabularyExample
Highest point peak at / reach a peak of / hit a high of "Sales peaked at 80,000 in 2010."
Lowest point hit a low of / bottom out / reach a trough of "Numbers hit a low of 12% in 1995."
No change remain stable / remain steady / level off / plateau "Figures remained stable throughout 2015."
Up and down fluctuate / a fluctuation "The data fluctuated considerably between 2000 and 2005."
Comparison while / whereas / compared to / in contrast "Country A saw a rise, whereas Country B declined."
Approximate figures approximately / roughly / just over / just under / nearly / around "Just under 40% of respondents preferred…"

Adverbs to Describe Degree of Change

Combine an adverb with a verb to show how dramatic the change was. This is essential for a high Lexical Resource score.

rose sharply increased dramatically declined gradually fell steadily grew marginally dropped significantly fluctuated slightly increased rapidly declined considerably

Useful Linking Phrases for Reports

  • Adding information: Furthermore, / In addition, / Moreover,
  • Contrasting: However, / In contrast, / While… / Whereas…
  • Showing sequence (diagrams): First, / Then, / After that, / Finally, / Subsequently,
  • Showing time: Between X and Y / From X to Y / During this period / By YEAR
  • Starting overview: Overall, / In general, / It is clear that / It is evident that
  • Comparing proportions: The largest share / The majority / A quarter of / More than half
⚠ Don't use "In conclusion" — this is a report, not an essay ⚠ Don't overuse "also" — use "furthermore" or "in addition" instead

Writing Practice Exercises

Exercise 1 — Paraphrase the Introduction

Read each question description below. Rewrite it as an introduction sentence using your own words. Do not copy. Change the vocabulary and sentence structure.

Paraphrase These Question Descriptions

  1. "The chart below shows the percentage of households with internet access in five countries in 2010 and 2020."
  2. "The diagram below shows how solar panels are used to provide electricity for domestic use."
  3. "The map below shows the changes that took place in the town of Riverside between 1980 and the present day."
  4. "The table below shows the number of students enrolled in four university subjects between 2015 and 2022."
Show Model Paraphrases
  1. "The bar chart illustrates the proportion of homes with internet connectivity across five nations, comparing figures from 2010 and 2020."
  2. "The diagram illustrates the process by which solar energy is converted into electricity for use in residential properties."
  3. "The maps compare the layout of Riverside town in 1980 with how it looks today, highlighting key changes that have occurred over the period."
  4. "The table provides data on student enrolment across four university disciplines over a seven-year period from 2015 to 2022."
Exercise 2 — Vocabulary Gap Fill (Line Graph)

The line graph shows the number of reported cases of influenza per 10,000 people in a city from 1985 to 2000. The number began at 40 in 1985 and then experienced a dramatic rise to 200 in 1990. After that, the number fell to 100 in 1993 before going up again to reach its highest point of 250 in 1997. From 1997 to 2000 there was a steady decline back to 150.

Fill in the gaps — Use NO MORE than two words for each answer

  1. The number of flu cases began at 40 in 1985, after which it ________ dramatically to reach 200 in 1990.
  2. There was then a ________ to 100 in 1993, before another upward turn.
  3. In 1997, cases ________ at 250, the highest point in the entire period.
  4. From 1997 onwards, the data showed a ________ decline, ending at 150 in 2000.
  5. Overall, flu cases ________ considerably across the fifteen-year period, ending significantly higher than they began.
Show Answers
  1. rose / climbed / increased
  2. fall / drop / decline
  3. peaked
  4. steady
  5. fluctuated
Exercise 3 — Write an Overview

The bar chart compares average monthly spending (USD) on five categories — Food, Transport, Entertainment, Clothing, and Healthcare — by two age groups: 18–30 and 50–65.

Key data:
  • 18–30 group spends most on Entertainment ($320) and least on Healthcare ($40)
  • 50–65 group spends most on Healthcare ($280) and least on Entertainment ($90)
  • Both groups spend similarly on Food (~$250 each)

Write a 2–3 sentence overview for this bar chart

Remember: no specific figures, general trends only, start with "Overall," or "In general,"

Ask yourself: What are the most obvious differences between the two groups? What does each group prioritise most?

Show Model Overview

"Overall, the two age groups show markedly different spending priorities. While younger adults allocate the greatest share of their budget to entertainment, older adults spend considerably more on healthcare. Food is the only category where spending is broadly similar across both groups."


  • No specific numbers ✓
  • Two clear contrasting trends identified ✓
  • One point of similarity noted ✓
  • Starts with "Overall," ✓
  • Good vocabulary: "markedly different", "allocate", "considerably", "broadly similar" ✓

Self-Assessment Checklist — After Writing a Full Task 1 Report

  1. Did I write at least 150 words?
  2. Did I paraphrase the question in my introduction (not copy it)?
  3. Did I write a clear overview as paragraph 2 with no specific figures?
  4. Did I cover all the key features?
  5. Did I include specific data (numbers, percentages, dates) in my body paragraphs?
  6. Did I use a range of vocabulary — trend verbs, adverbs, comparatives?
  7. Did I use the correct tense for the data shown?
  8. Did I avoid giving my opinion?
  9. Did I write NO conclusion?
  10. Did I check spelling of key vocabulary?

Examiner Tips

  • Read what you have written Go back and read the paragraph you have just written before you start the next one. You may think that this is a waste of time. If so, you’d be wrong. It’s important to link your paragraphs together â what easier way to do that than just read what you have written?
  • Be clear The practical advice here is to select your best idea and write about that. That means not writing everything you know â leave some ideas out. Don’t worry if it is not your best explanation, worry about whether it is your clearest explanation.
  • Write about what you know You are being tested on the quality of your English, not on the quality of your ideas. So you shouldn’t worry about finding the “right answer”. You need a simple idea that you can clearly describe and justify.
  • Introduction : briefly describe what your graph shows.
  • Overview : state main trends.
  • Specific details : describe specific changes, providing data.

The first graph shows the cultural and leisure activities that boys participate in, whereas the second graph shows the activities in which the girls participate. Reveal model answer

You should spend about 20 minutes on this task.

The pie graphs below show the result of a survey of children's activities. The first graph shows the cultural and leisure activities that boys participate in, whereas the second graph shows the activities in which the girls participate.

Write a report describing the information shown in the two pie graphs.

Tell about the activities on the boys' chart and their popularity. Use linking structures and vocabulary to describe graphs .

Tell about the activities on the girls' chart and their popularity. Use words from academic wordlist .

The two pie charts draw the conclusion of a survey of boys' and girls' cultural and leisure activities.

Overall, equal quantities of both sexes enjoyed listening to music, but a dramatically larger number of girls liked reading. There were also many differences in terms of the children's preferred sports.

Turning to the first chart, we can observe that boys prefer playing computer games (34% participation rate) than taking other activities. Playing basketball comes as the second most popular leisure, practiced by almost a third of male children. Basketball is followed by soccer, which is exercised by 17%. Skateboarding and listening to music are less preferable activities, chosen by 11% and 10% of boys respectively. Reading, the least popular cultural activity among boys, represents only two percent.
