Task 2 · Essay

Opinion Essays

What is it?

The most common Task 2 type. A statement is given and you are asked "To what extent do you agree or disagree?" or "Do you agree or disagree?". You must state your own position and support it for at least 250 words.

The Golden Rule

  • Your position must be clear in the introduction and consistent to the end
  • "To what extent" invites a strong (completely agree) or partial (agree, but…) position — both work, but a partial position needs more skill to keep consistent
  • You do not have to present both sides — one well-defended side scores higher than a wobbly balance
  • Ideas are not graded — clarity of argument is. Pick your most explainable idea, not your cleverest.

Structure (4 paragraphs)

  1. Introduction: paraphrase the statement (sentence 1) + your position (sentence 2): "While some argue…, I firmly believe…"
  2. Body 1: first reason. Topic sentence → explanation → example (TEE).
  3. Body 2: second reason, same TEE shape. (A partial-agreement essay uses this paragraph for the concession: "Admittedly…, however…")
  4. Conclusion: restate the position in fresh words. No new ideas.

Example question

Some people believe that modern technology is making people less sociable. To what extent do you agree or disagree?
Show model introduction & body paragraph

It is often claimed that today's devices are eroding our ability to connect with one another. I largely disagree with this view: although technology has changed how we socialise, it has expanded rather than reduced genuine human contact.

The main reason is that technology removes the geographical limits that once defined a person's social world. Video calls, group chats and online communities allow relationships to survive relocation, migration and busy schedules that would previously have ended them. My own grandparents, for example, speak to relatives abroad every week — conversations that a generation ago would have been reduced to one expensive phone call a year.


  • Position clear by the end of the introduction, and it is a nuanced one ✓
  • Body: topic sentence → explanation → concrete example (TEE) ✓
  • Paraphrase quality: less sociable → eroding our ability to connect ✓

Common Mistakes

⚠ No clear position — describing both sides and never choosing ⚠ Position flips between introduction and conclusion ⚠ Answering a different question than the one asked ⚠ New arguments appearing in the conclusion
