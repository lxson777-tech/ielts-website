About the Test

IELTS Reading at a Glance

The Basics

  • Total time: 60 minutes — unlike Listening, there is no extra transfer time
  • 40 questions across 3 passages, 1 mark each
  • Around 2,150–2,750 words of text in total
  • Passages increase in difficulty from Passage 1 to Passage 3
  • No negative marking — never leave a blank, always guess
  • Spelling and grammar count: a misspelled answer is marked wrong

The Three Passages

  • Passage 1 — easiest. Descriptive, factual text (e.g. a process, a place, an invention). Aim for speed here.
  • Passage 2 — medium. Discursive text, often work- or training-related, with more paraphrasing.
  • Passage 3 — hardest. Argumentative, abstract academic text with opinion-based questions.
  • Budget roughly 20 minutes per passage, then move on — the easy marks in a later passage are worth more than one stubborn question.
  • Texts come from books, journals, magazines and newspapers — no specialist knowledge is needed; every answer is in the text.
