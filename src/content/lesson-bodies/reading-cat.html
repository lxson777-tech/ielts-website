Categorisation / Classification

Categorisation Questions

What is it?

Also called Classification or Matching Features. You are given a list of statements and a set of 2–3 categories (A, B, C). You must decide which category each statement belongs to, based on information in the passage. Multiple statements can share the same category.

Key Rules

  • Your answer is always a letter
  • Multiple questions can have the same letter as an answer
  • The categories are often people, places, time periods, or groups
  • The passage decides which category — use only what's in the text

How to Approach It

  1. Read the categories carefully — understand what each one represents.
  2. Skim the passage to identify which section refers to each category.
  3. Read each statement and identify keywords.
  4. Locate the relevant passage section and decide which category the information belongs to.
  5. Don't panic if the same letter appears several times — that's normal.
⚠ Trap: using general knowledge — rely only on the passage ⚠ Trap: assuming each category is used only once
Passage: Three Ancient Civilisations

The Egyptians were one of the most technologically advanced ancient civilisations. They developed a writing system called hieroglyphics, built massive stone monuments including the pyramids, and created a complex system of mathematics and astronomy. Their society was ruled by a god-king known as a Pharaoh, and religion was central to daily life. Embalming the dead and building elaborate tombs were key practices.

The Greeks made extraordinary contributions to philosophy, science, and democracy. Greek thinkers such as Socrates, Plato, and Aristotle developed ideas that still influence modern thought. The Greeks introduced the concept of democracy in Athens, constructed architectural masterpieces like the Parthenon, and founded the Olympic Games as a religious and athletic festival.

The Romans were famous for their military power and engineering skill. They built an extensive network of roads that connected their empire, constructed aqueducts to supply water to cities, and developed a legal system that forms the basis of many modern laws. Roman culture absorbed many elements of Greek philosophy and religion, and Latin — the Roman language — is the root of several modern European languages.
