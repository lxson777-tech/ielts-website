Matching Paragraph Information

What is it?

You are given a list of statements. Each statement contains a specific piece of information. You must identify which labelled paragraph (A, B, C…) contains that information. This is about locating specific details — not the main idea of the paragraph.

vs. Matching Headings

  • Matching Headings — What is the paragraph mainly about?
  • Matching Paragraphs — Which paragraph contains this specific detail?
  • One paragraph can be the answer to more than one question
  • Your answer is always a letter

How to Approach It

  1. Read all the statements first and identify keywords and paraphrases.
  2. Skim the passage to get a sense of what each paragraph covers.
  3. For each statement, scan the passage for keywords or synonyms.
  4. When you find the relevant section, confirm it contains the information in the statement.
  5. Remember: a paragraph can answer more than one question — check the instructions.
⚠ Trap: confusing this with Matching Headings ⚠ Trap: forgetting that one letter can be used more than once
Passage: Beethoven

A. Composer Ludwig van Beethoven was born on or near December 16, 1770, in Bonn, Germany. He is widely considered the greatest composer of all time. Sometime between the births of his two younger brothers, Beethoven's father began teaching him music with an extraordinary rigour and brutality, which affected him for the rest of his life. On a near daily basis, Beethoven was flogged, locked in the cellar and deprived of sleep for extra hours of practice.

B. In 1804, Beethoven was completing his third symphony, which he at first named "Bonaparte" in honour of Napoleon. However, when later that year Napoleon proclaimed himself Emperor, Beethoven was so disappointed that he renamed his composition "Eroica". This work debuted in Vienna in 1805. It was so unlike anything heard before that through weeks of rehearsal, the musicians could not figure out how to play it.

C. At the same time as he was composing these great works, Beethoven was trying hard to come to terms with a shocking fact that he tried desperately to conceal. He was going deaf. At the turn of the century, Beethoven struggled to make out the words spoken to him in conversation.

D. Despite his extraordinary output of beautiful music, Beethoven was frequently miserable throughout his adult life. Beethoven died on March 26, 1827, at the age of 56.
