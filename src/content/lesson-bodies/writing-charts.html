Task 1 · Report

Charts, Graphs & Tables

What is it?

You are given a bar chart, line graph, pie chart or table (sometimes two combined) and must summarise the information in at least 150 words. You select and compare the key features — you do not list every number, and you never give an opinion.

What the Examiner Wants

  • A paraphrased introduction — never copy the question
  • A clear overview of the main trends, with no specific figures
  • Body paragraphs that group logically and quote data selectively
  • The correct tense for the period shown (past years → past tense)
  • No conclusion, no opinion — this is a report

The Four-Paragraph Structure

  1. Introduction (1 sentence): paraphrase what the chart shows — change the vocabulary and sentence structure.
  2. Overview (2–3 sentences): the biggest trends and contrasts, starting with "Overall,". No numbers. This is the most important paragraph for your Task Achievement band.
  3. Body 1: the first logical group of data (e.g. the categories that rose), with specific figures.
  4. Body 2: the remaining group (e.g. the categories that fell), with specific figures and comparisons.

Trend Language

DirectionVerbsNouns

Grade the change with adverbs: slightly, gradually, steadily, considerably, sharply, dramatically. For proportions: a quarter of, more than half, the vast majority, one in five.

Example question

The chart below shows the percentage of households with internet access in five countries in 2010 and 2020. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.
Show model report (Band 8+ features)

The bar chart compares the proportion of homes with an internet connection across five nations in 2010 and 2020.

Overall, connectivity rose in every country over the decade, but the scale of the change differed enormously: the countries that started lowest grew fastest, considerably narrowing the gap with the early adopters.

In 2010, the two wealthiest countries already enjoyed widespread access, at 81% and 76% respectively, and these figures edged up to around 95% by 2020. Growth here was steady but modest, since most households were already online at the start of the period.

The remaining three countries present a strikingly different picture. Access in the lowest-ranked nation more than tripled, jumping from just 18% to 61%, while the other two roughly doubled their figures to reach 70% and 74%. As a result, by 2020 no country in the group had less than three-fifths of its households connected, compared with fewer than one in five a decade earlier.


  • Introduction paraphrases (shows → compares; percentage of households → proportion of homes) ✓
  • Overview states the two big trends, no figures ✓
  • Bodies group the data logically (leaders vs catchers-up), not country by country ✓
  • Selective figures with comparative language throughout ✓

Common Mistakes

⚠ Copying the question wording into the introduction ⚠ No overview — an instant Task Achievement ceiling of Band 5 ⚠ Listing every data point instead of selecting key features ⚠ Writing a conclusion or giving an opinion ⚠ Describing categories one by one with no comparison
