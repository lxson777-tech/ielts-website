Topic Vocabulary

Artificial Intelligence

Word / PhraseMeaningExample in Context
machine learninga branch of AI in which systems improve automatically by learning from data"Machine learning algorithms can identify fraudulent transactions in real time."
generative AIAI systems capable of producing new text, images, or other content from a prompt"Generative AI tools can now draft an entire essay in seconds."
algorithma set of rules a computer follows to complete a task or solve a problem"Streaming platforms use algorithms to recommend content based on viewing habits."
automationthe use of technology to perform tasks with minimal human involvement"Automation has eliminated many repetitive roles in manufacturing."
job displacementthe loss of employment caused by technological change"Economists warn that job displacement will accelerate as AI adoption grows."
reskillingtraining workers in new skills to help them adapt to a changing job market"Governments are funding reskilling programmes to prepare workers for an AI-driven economy."
autonomous vehiclea vehicle capable of operating without human control, using AI and sensors"Autonomous vehicles promise to reduce accidents caused by human error."
algorithmic biassystematic errors in AI outputs that unfairly favour or disadvantage particular groups"Algorithmic bias in hiring software can inadvertently discriminate against certain applicants."
human oversightthe practice of having people review or control decisions made by AI systems"Many experts argue that human oversight should remain mandatory in high-stakes AI decisions."
chatbota program that simulates conversation with users through text or voice"Many companies now use chatbots to handle routine customer service enquiries."

Key Collocations

  • deploy / adopt artificial intelligence
  • automate a task / a process
  • displace workers / jobs
  • mitigate the risks of AI
  • regulate the use of AI
  • a double-edged sword

Useful Essay Phrases

  • "While AI undoubtedly boosts efficiency, it also raises concerns about…"
  • "The rapid pace of automation has outstripped our ability to…"
  • "Proponents argue that AI will create as many jobs as it destroys."
  • "Without adequate regulation, the risks of AI could outweigh its benefits."
  • "Rather than resisting automation, workers should be equipped to…"
Show Practice Exercise

Fill in the gaps using words from this section:

  1. Many companies are turning to ________ tools to generate marketing copy in seconds. (generative AI)
  2. Widespread ________ in manufacturing has reduced the need for manual labour. (automation)
  3. Critics argue that ________ in recruitment software can unfairly disadvantage certain groups. (algorithmic bias)
  4. Governments are investing in ________ programmes to help displaced workers find new careers. (reskilling)
  5. Experts insist that ________ must remain in place for high-stakes AI decisions, such as medical diagnoses. (human oversight)
