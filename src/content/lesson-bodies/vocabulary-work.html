Topic Vocabulary

Work & Employment

Word / PhraseMeaningExample in Context
gig economya labour market characterised by short-term, freelance, or on-demand work rather than permanent jobs"The gig economy has given workers flexibility but often at the cost of job security."
work-life balancethe equilibrium between time spent on work and personal life"Employees increasingly prioritise work-life balance over salary when choosing a job."
job satisfactionthe level of contentment employees feel about their work"Job satisfaction is closely linked to autonomy and a sense of purpose."
redundancythe loss of a job because it is no longer needed by an employer"Thousands of workers faced redundancy after the factory's automation upgrade."
minimum wagethe lowest hourly pay an employer is legally permitted to pay"Raising the minimum wage remains a contentious economic policy."
four-day work weeka working schedule that compresses full-time hours into four days instead of five"Trials of the four-day work week have reported improved productivity and wellbeing."
employee burnouta state of chronic physical and emotional exhaustion caused by prolonged work stress"Employee burnout has become a growing concern in high-pressure industries."
career progressionthe advancement of an employee through increasingly senior roles"Limited career progression is a common reason employees leave a company."
unemployment ratethe percentage of the labour force that is without work but seeking employment"The unemployment rate rose sharply following the economic downturn."
workplace diversitythe inclusion of people from a wide range of backgrounds within an organisation"Companies with strong workplace diversity often report higher levels of innovation."

Useful Essay Phrases

  • "Younger generations increasingly value flexibility over long-term job security."
  • "Employers who fail to address burnout risk losing their most talented staff."
  • "The rise of the gig economy has blurred the line between employment and self-employment."
  • "A shorter working week could improve productivity without sacrificing output."
  • "Job security alone is no longer enough to guarantee employee satisfaction."
Show Practice Exercise

Fill in the gaps using words from this section:

  1. Many young professionals now prioritise ________ over a higher salary. (work-life balance)
  2. Several companies trialling a ________ reported no drop in overall output. (four-day work week)
  3. Prolonged overwork without adequate rest can eventually lead to ________. (employee burnout)
  4. Rising automation has led to widespread ________ in the manufacturing sector. (redundancy)
  5. Freelance platforms have fuelled rapid growth in the ________ over the past decade. (gig economy)
