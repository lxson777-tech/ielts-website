Topic Vocabulary

Social Media & Digital Life

Word / PhraseMeaningExample in Context
echo chamberan environment in which a person only encounters opinions that reinforce their own"Critics argue that social media algorithms trap users in echo chambers."
cyberbullyingthe use of digital communication to harass, threaten, or intimidate someone"Schools are introducing new policies to tackle cyberbullying among students."
screen timethe amount of time spent using a device with a screen"Excessive screen time has been linked to poor sleep quality in teenagers."
digital footprintthe trail of data a person leaves behind through online activity"Employers increasingly check a candidate's digital footprint before making a job offer."
influencera person who has built a large online following and can affect others' opinions or buying habits"Influencer marketing has become one of the most profitable branches of advertising."
viral contentmaterial that spreads rapidly across the internet through sharing"A single piece of viral content can reach millions of people within hours."
fear of missing out (FOMO)anxiety that others are having rewarding experiences from which one is absent"FOMO drives many young users to check social media compulsively throughout the day."
digital detoxa deliberate period of abstaining from digital devices, especially social media"More people are choosing a digital detox to improve their mental wellbeing."
clickbaitonline content, often a headline, designed to attract attention and encourage clicks rather than inform"Many news outlets have been criticised for relying on clickbait headlines."
online identitythe persona a person presents and maintains across digital platforms"Teenagers often experiment with different aspects of their online identity."

Key Collocations

  • go viral
  • curate an online persona
  • fall victim to misinformation
  • foster / erode social connection
  • set boundaries around screen time
  • a constant stream of content

Useful Essay Phrases

  • "It is widely argued that social media has a mostly negative effect on young people."
  • "While social media fosters connection, it can also exacerbate feelings of isolation."
  • "The curated nature of online content often distorts users' sense of reality."
  • "Platforms have a responsibility to address the psychological effects of their algorithms."
  • "Rather than banning social media outright, education on responsible use would be more effective."
Show Practice Exercise

Fill in the gaps using words from this section:

  1. Many users feel trapped in an ________, only ever seeing opinions that match their own. (echo chamber)
  2. A growing number of people are taking a ________ over the summer to reduce their reliance on their phones. (digital detox)
  3. News websites are frequently criticised for using ________ headlines to drive traffic. (clickbait)
  4. Employers now routinely check a candidate's ________ before extending a job offer. (digital footprint)
  5. Psychologists say that ________ is a major driver of compulsive social media use among teenagers. (FOMO / fear of missing out)
